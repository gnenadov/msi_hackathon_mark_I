Month Day - Month Day

Join us and help provide relief for those affected by COVID-19.

What is this?

Hack! MSI is a virtual hackathon open to all students in the Michigan School of Information.

Our aim is to tackle some of the challenges related to the current outbreak of coronavirus across the globe.

We're asking students to produce tools to help people work from home, provide them with more information about outbreaks in their areas, help inform people on sanitary guidelines they should be following, and more.

Main Themes

Working Remotely

The world is currently facing unprecendented challenges. One of those many challenges is adapting practically every professional workplace to operate remotely. We want to help ease that transition and reduce the challenges workers face performing their jobs at home.

Spread Awareness and Information

The people of the world are scared of one another. The coronavirus pandemic grows worse by the day, with cases breaking out in different cities every hour. With so many outbreaks it's hard to stay on top of the news. We want to help provide people across the world with information that they can use to protect themselves.

Got Questions?

What's a hackathon?

A hackathon is a competition where teams compete to create tools/prototypes that solve a problem or answer a challenge. This particular hackathon will be 100% virtual.

What if I don't have a team?

Solo projects are 100% accepted, we don't want to stand in your way. We also have a Discord server where students can look for teammates!

How do I work with my team?

Working remotely is one of the many challenges the world is facing right now. Unfortunately this will impact our hackathon. Do your best to communicate with your team!

What timezone are we using?

As this event is for students of the University of Michigan, we will be using the EST timezone.
